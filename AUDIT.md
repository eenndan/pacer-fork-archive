# Pacer Repository Audit

> Generated by a multi-agent swarm (deep finders -> adversarial code-verification -> orchestrator synthesis). Ranked by the project's #1 priority — **LLM-editability & structure** — then correctness, dead code, efficiency, comments, tests.

## Executive summary

**178 verified findings** across the production code (`pacer/` C++ core, `bindings/`, `studio/` app, `tests/`). Severity: **0 critical · 13 high · 54 medium · 111 low**. Category: structure 46, bugs 42, dead/redundant 43, inefficiency 24, comments 13, tests 10. Area: cpp 63, studio-core 44, studio-views 40, tests 14, bindings 6, dev/tmp 10.

**Headline themes.** (1) The C++ `gps-source` and `laps` modules hold the few genuinely behavioral bugs — a `uint32_t` underflow in `Seek`, a GPS5<->GPS9 speed-field swap, an un-freed per-payload GPMF buffer, and a chaptered-source first-payload skip. (2) The biggest *structural* wins are splitting the ~360-line GPS pipeline out of `session.py` and the 223-line per-codec `GPMFSource::Samples`, plus tightening the 'views stay pacer-free' boundary (a few views reach through `session.laps` / private attrs). (3) A long tail of safe, mechanical cleanups (dead code, unused symbols, stale comments) and ~10 dev/tmp delete-candidates. (4) Cheap perf wins on the 30 Hz tick (memoize `valid_lap_ids()`, coalesce map-drag seeks).
